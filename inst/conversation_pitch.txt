The Eurovision Song Contest is an international song competition that
debuted in 1956 as a way for a Europe divided by war to come together
to celebrate music.  Every year several dozen countries across Europe
and beyond send a representative song to compete.  The Eurovision Song
Contest has a continuing significant cultural impact not only within
Europe but the rest of the world.  As a cultural institution, we would
hope for the Eurovision Song Contest to be as fair as possible.

In 2022, six voting juries were accused of collusion: it seemed that
these six juries had agreed ahead of time to award one another top
marks for their entries.  The ensuing scandal was unprecedented: for
the first time ever, multiple juries in one year had their scores
nullified.  The accused juries consistently denied any wrongdoing and
lamented the lack of transparency in the reasoning behind removing the
scores.  To date, no open formal statistical analysis is available on
the resulting data.

We use statistical techniques originally developed for competitive
cooking competitions such as MasterChef Australia, to assess the
scandal.  We find overwhelmingly strong evidence to support the
decision to nullify the six juries' scores.
