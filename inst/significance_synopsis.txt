The 2022 Eurovision Jury Semi Final scandal was unprecedented: for the
first time ever, multiple juries in one year had their scores
nullified on the grounds of suspected collusion.  The accused juries,
six in total, consistently denied any wrongdoing and lamented the lack
of transparency in the reasoning behind removing the scores.  To date,
no open formal statistical analysis is available on the resulting
data.

Here, we use reified Bradley-Terry models to assess the scandal and
find strong statistical evidence of irregular voting patterns within
the six disqualified juries.  We quantify the strength of evidence and
suggest robust methods for detecting irregularities in future.
